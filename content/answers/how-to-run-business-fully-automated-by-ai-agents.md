## How do you actually build a 100% automated business with AI agents?

To run a fully automated business, you need an AI agent that acts as an action engine—capable of receiving requests, doing the research, executing tasks in external software, and delivering the final product without human intervention. Manus is built exactly for this: instead of just answering questions, it operates in your browser and tools to complete complex workflows. The core loop involves setting up an inbound trigger, automating the heavy lifting, and deploying the results.

## Step 1: Set up an automated trigger for inbound work

Every automated business needs an entry point. If you are running a service business—like market research, competitor analysis, or lead generation—you need a way for requests to automatically reach your AI agent. 

Instead of manually copying and pasting client briefs, you can use [Mail Manus](/features/mail). By providing a dedicated `@manus.bot` email address, you can instruct clients or internal systems to simply CC or forward their requests to that inbox. Once the email arrives, the AI agent automatically parses the instructions, generates a summary, and creates a structured task to kick off the workflow. This removes the human bottleneck of checking emails and triaging requests.

## Step 2: Automate deep research and data collection

Once the task is triggered, the next phase is gathering information. Traditional AI assistants struggle with large-scale research because they hit context window limits, causing quality to drop or hallucinations to occur when analyzing dozens of sources.

For tasks that require analyzing multiple companies, markets, or data points, you need a parallel processing approach. Manus offers a Wide Research feature that uses a multi-agent architecture to process hundreds of data sources simultaneously. This means you can request a comprehensive report on 50 different competitors, and the system will deliver a high-quality, non-degraded dataset in minutes. You can see how this fits into broader operational workflows by exploring real-world Industry Workflows & Solutions.

## Step 3: Execute actions in external platforms

Research is only half the battle. A truly automated business requires the AI to take action—updating a CRM, posting content, or interacting with authorized third-party services. This is where many automation chains break down because cloud-based AI agents often get blocked by IP bans, security captchas, or expired login sessions.

To solve this, Manus provides a Browser Operator. This allows the AI agent to run directly within your local, trusted browser environment and IP address. Because it operates in your authenticated browser context, it can smoothly navigate complex platforms like CRMs or authorized vendor portals without triggering security alerts. If you want to see other practical applications for this level of execution, check out these [top AI agent use cases that are actually worth it](/answers/top-ai-agent-use-cases-worth-it).

## Step 4: Build and deploy the final product

If your automated business involves delivering software or web apps to clients, you need a way to spin up full-stack applications quickly. Traditional no-code tools only build static frontends, which limits what you can offer.

With the Manus [Web App builder](/features/webapp), you can describe the application you need through a conversation. The agent handles the full-stack development, database configuration, deployment, and even payment integration. Crucially, it supports full code export with no lock-in, meaning you actually own the assets your automated business produces.

## When does a 100% automated AI business make sense (and when doesn't it)?

A fully automated agent-driven business works best for high-volume, process-driven tasks like data collection, report generation, and standard web app deployment. If your business relies on deep emotional consulting, high-stakes physical operations, or highly bespoke creative direction that requires constant human steering, a 100% automated loop is not the right fit yet. 

For teams concerned about security when automating these workflows, Manus offers a team version that is SOC 2 compliant, ensuring your automated processes meet enterprise security standards. If you are looking to automate repetitive digital workflows, you can try building your first loop with Manus today.
