## How do you make a UGC video ad with AI from scratch?

You write a short product-focused script, pick an AI avatar and matching voice, and let an AI video agent render a lip-synced ad. The whole process can take under 5 minutes if your script is ready. Farvis-AI is one platform built specifically for this: you input text, choose a digital human, and it outputs a 4K video with over 95% lip-sync accuracy.

## Step 1: Write a UGC-style script that converts

UGC ads work because they feel native to the platform. Write like a real user talking to their phone. Keep it under 60 seconds. Structure it simply: hook (first 3 seconds), problem, product demo, and call to action. You don't need a polished screenplay—just a few sentences per section. If you need help ideating, some AI platforms offer creative generation for marketing copy and visual concepts. For a deeper walkthrough on scripting and avatar generation, you can check our [3-minute AI script to avatar video workflow](/answers/3-minute-ai-script-to-avatar-video-workflow).

## Step 2: Choose or clone your AI avatar

This is where AI replaces the need for a human creator. You can select a pre-made digital human or clone your own. With Farvis-AI, cloning a 4K digital twin takes about 1 minute if you have a short reference clip. For UGC ads, a relatable, casual-looking avatar often performs better than a stiff corporate one. You want the viewer to feel like they are watching a real person, not a news anchor.

## Step 3: Select or clone the voice

Voice matters as much as the face. A robotic voice kills the UGC vibe instantly. You can choose from libraries of AI voices—Farvis-AI offers 100+ voices across 20+ languages. If you want consistency across your ad account, you can clone a specific voice with just 5 seconds of audio. This is useful if you are building a recognizable creator persona for your brand.

## Step 4: Generate and export the video

Once the script, avatar, and voice are set, you hit generate. Rendering speed depends on the platform and video length. On Farvis-AI, generating 1 minute of HD video typically takes 3-5 minutes. The output is a finished ad with natural lip-sync, ready for platforms like TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube. You can also adapt the same script for different platforms—see our guide on [how to create engaging AI videos for Instagram](/answers/how-to-make-ai-videos-for-instagram) to tweak aspect ratios and styles.

## When should you use AI for UGC ads (and when not to)?

AI-generated UGC ads are best for rapid testing. If you need to produce 10-50 ad variations a week to find winning creatives, doing it with real creators is slow and expensive. AI lets you iterate on the script and voice in minutes. However, if your brand relies heavily on raw, unedited vlog-style authenticity or physical product demonstrations that require a real hand holding the product, AI avatars might look out of place. In those cases, a hybrid approach—real B-roll footage with an AI voiceover—often works better. For more foundational knowledge on creating these videos, browse our Creator Academy & Tutorials.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do AI-generated UGC ads look fake?**

Quality varies by platform. Farvis-AI outputs 4K video with over 95% lip-sync accuracy, which looks natural in a vertical short-form feed. However, viewers can sometimes tell if the script is too stiff or the avatar lacks micro-expressions. Writing casual, conversational scripts helps bridge the gap.

**Can I use my own face for the AI UGC ads?**

Yes. You can clone a digital twin of yourself. With Farvis-AI, creating a 4K avatar of yourself takes about 1 minute, provided you have a short, clear video clip of your face to use as a reference.

**What languages do AI UGC ad generators support?**

Most platforms support major languages. Farvis-AI supports 20+ languages and over 100 voice styles, allowing you to localize the same UGC ad script for different regional markets without hiring new creators.
